This Cookie Policy explains how View Passion Co., Ltd. uses cookies and similar technologies (SDKs, pixels, local storage) across our games (including Cat vs Cucumber), our studio and game websites, and related services. By using our Services you agree to the use of cookies as described; you can manage or withdraw consent anytime via your device or browser settings.

Cat vs Cucumber & every View Passion title. This policy covers our games and websites studio-wide, including Cat vs Cucumber. For the game-specific version, see the Cat vs Cucumber Cookie Policy.

01What are cookies?

Cookies are small files stored on your device that help remember actions and preferences. They may be first-party (set by us) or third-party (set by partners). In apps we use equivalent technologies such as SDKs, pixels, and local storage.

02Why we use them

  • Functionality and security of our games and websites.
  • Remembering preferences (language, audio).
  • Analytics and performance.
  • Personalized content and advertising, and campaign measurement.

03Types of cookies

CategoryPurpose
Strictly necessaryLogin, saving progress, core functionality. Cannot be turned off.
PerformanceUnderstand usage and technical issues (e.g., Firebase, GA4).
FunctionalRemember preferences and enable enhanced features.

05Managing your preferences

Web: manage cookies in your browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox) to block, allow, or delete them — note that disabling strictly necessary cookies may affect functionality. Mobile: Android — Settings > Google > Ads; iOS — Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking. Where applicable we honor the Global Privacy Control (GPC) signal, and consent prompts are shown where required.

By audience

  • Players (in-app): manage ad personalization and tracking through your device settings and any in-game prompts.
  • Website visitors: manage cookies in your browser; decline non-essential cookies where a consent prompt is shown.
  • Business & B2B visitors: our studio site may use LinkedIn Insight for B2B marketing measurement, controllable the same way.

06Retention

Session cookies are deleted when your session ends; persistent cookies last a set period (typically 30–365 days).
